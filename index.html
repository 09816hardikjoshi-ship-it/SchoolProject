Physical & Chemical Changes

Understanding Matter Transformations



















Physical Change










Definition :-

A physical change is a transformation that alters the form or appearance of a substance but does not change its fundamental chemical composition or identity.














Properties of Physical Change








      → No New Substance Formed


      → Usually Reversible


      → Change in Physical Properties Only


      → Minimal Energy Change


      → Molecular Rearrangement












Chemical Change










Definition :-

A chemical change, or a chemical reaction is a fundamental process that results in the formation of one or more new substances with distinct chemical compositions and physical properties from the original starting materials.














Properties of Chemical Change








      → Formation of New Substance


      → Usually Reversible


      → Significant Energy Change


      → Changes in Chemical Properties


      → Atmoic Rearrangement










Indicators of Physical Change




  • No new chemical identity


  • Reversibility


  • Change limited to state/shape


  • Minimal energy signature


  • No unexpected color change









Indicators of Chemical Change




  • Color Change


  • Temperatue Change


  • Formation of a Gas/Bubbles


  • Formation of a precipitate


  • Production of light, sound or odor








Key Differences



Physical Changes

No new substance formed. Shape,size,or state changes only.

Chemical Changes

New substance formed. Atoms rearrange to create different matter.




Physical Change Examples




  • Dissolving Salt in Water


  • Making Alloys


  • Sublimation of Iodine


  • Magnetizing and Demagnetizing Iron


























Chemical Change Examples




  • Cooking and burning sugar


  • Rusting vs simple corrosion of metals


  • Milk turning into curd


  • Burning a candle
